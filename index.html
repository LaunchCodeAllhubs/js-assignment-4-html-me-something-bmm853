Highlights of Portland, Maine

When my friends and I were first bouncing around the idea of going on a trip to celebrate a year in which we would all be reaching a milestone birthday, we had no idea we would wind up living out an idyllic dream vacation, fulfilling our mutual desires to act like sea-captains and spend time together experiencing the interesting history of Portland, Maine.

The Sea

One of the biggest draws of Portland is that it abuts the Atlantic Ocean, allowing for stunning views and boating activities.

Whale Watching

In true sea-captain fashion, we went on a whale-seeking quest with decidedly less murderous intentions than the famous Ahab. Unfortunately, we only saw the back half of a whale from quite a distance. Whales are fickle and illusive creatures.

Peak's Island

Another highlight of the trip was ferrying to Peak's Island where we climbed onto the many rocky becahes to view waves crashing into the rocks.

Lighthouses

When the initial idea of going to Maine was proposed, I excitedly exclaimed, "I wanna see some lighthouses!" This became the motto of our trip, and, thankfully, there are many lighthouses to see.

Portland Head Light

This stunning lighthouse attracts many tourists but is adjacent to a 90-acre park that allows opportunities to space out and enjoy the gorgeous views.

The Bug

The Portland Breakwater Light, known as "The Bug" due to its tiny stature, is only 26 feet tall.

Spring Point Ledge Lighthouse

Jutting into the harbor from its home on a community college campus, visitors can walk out on the breakwater to access the lighthouse and its scenic magic.

Ram Island Ledge Light

Built in 1905, Ram Island Ledge Light is the newest lighthouse in the area.

Embracing History

Portland is full of reminders of years past, whether 20 or 200 years ago.

Old Port

The cobblestone-lined streets of Old Port are a reminder of the pre-revolutionary history of Portland, Maine. Some buildings still have shutters on their windows that originally served to stop the bowsprit of ships from crashing through the glass panes. Suffering two massive fires, the city has managed to rebuild and modernize while maintaining its historic charm.

Victoria Mansion

Built as a summer home by a former Mainer who moved to the south to beome a hotelier and investor in the slave trade, Victoria Mansion serves as a reminder that the North was not immune to the influence of slavery and racism. The mansion is not done in the Victorian architectural style but was named after Queen Victoria in an attempt to secure funding for repairs to the home. The family was sent a painting instead.

Vintage Shops

There are several fun vintage shops in Portland. One in particular focused on items from the 90s and one of my friends found the same dress she wore to prom there. It really drove home the fact that we were in Portland to celebrate getting older. Thanks, Portland.
